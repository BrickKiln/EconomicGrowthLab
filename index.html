The Harrod-Domar Growth Model

“One of the most fundamental strategies of economic growth is simply to increase the proportion of national income saved (i.e., not consumed).”

The rate of growth of GDP is determined joinly by the net national savings ratio and the national-capital output ratio

Ready for some math?

Net saving ($S$) = portion of national income ($Y$)

$S = sY$ (so $s$ = saving ratio)

Net investment($I$) = change in capital stock($K$)

$$I = \Delta K$$

$K$ relates to $Y$ by capital-output ratio ($c$)

$$\frac{K}{Y} = c \ ... or ...\ \frac{\Delta K}{\Delta Y} = c$$
$$\Rightarrow \Delta K = c\Delta Y$$

Assuming saving = investment

$$S = I$$

So far..

$$I = \Delta K = c\Delta Y$$

But $S = I$ and $S = sY$

$$\Rightarrow sY = c\Delta Y$$

Dividing by $Y$ and then by $c$

$$\frac{\Delta Y}{Y} = \frac{s}{c}$$
